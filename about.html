About Me

A Journey of Curiosity and Innovation

Curious Beginnings
Growing up in India, I was surrounded by diverse cultures and rich traditions. My curiosity about the world began early, sparked by childhood observations and a love for solving puzzles. One defining moment came in high school when I noticed that my cousins and I often fell sick after visiting my grandparents. With the guidance of my biology teacher, I collected water samples, performed tests, and discovered high levels of E. coli in the local water supply. This discovery led to a community awareness campaign promoting water purifiers, instilling in me a passion for tackling real-world problems.

Diving into Engineering
Driven by my interest in problem-solving, I pursued a B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering at PES University. During my studies, I explored signal processing and machine learning, undertaking impactful projects like an IoT-enabled multimodal data fusion system for sensor reliability and compression. These experiences shaped my fascination with integrating diverse data sources to solve complex problems.

Aiding Frontline Workers During COVID-19
The pandemic marked a turning point in my journey. As an IEEE intern, I remodeled CheXNet to detect COVID-19 from X-rays and CT scans, fine-tuning it to reduce false negatives. Seeing my model adopted by a radiology lab to support frontline workers filled me with pride and deepened my interest in biomedical signal processing. However, the challenges of “black box” AI models pushed me to seek deeper insights and more robust solutions.

From Industry to Academia
After completing my undergraduate degree, I worked at Amazon, where I honed my skills in software engineering and cloud technologies. I developed scalable full-stack applications, shadow testing frameworks, and CI/CD pipelines. While I enjoyed the technical challenges, my passion to learn more and to not leave things unknown led me back to academia, where I could merge my skills to address pressing healthcare problems.

Innovating at Michigan
Now pursuing a master’s in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Michigan, I focus on combining signal processing, machine learning, and precision medicine. My research includes modeling Alzheimer's progression using a Conditional Mixture of Gaussians VAE, building probabilistic frameworks for neural and behavioral data, and enhancing the biological plausibility of generative models. Each project deepens my understanding and motivates me to create solutions that matter.
